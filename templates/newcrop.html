Soil Requirements

{% if result == 'rice' %}

Rice grows best in clayey loam soil with good water retention capacity. Ideal pH range: 5.5-6.5.

{% elif result == 'maize' %}

Maize requires well-drained loamy soil with pH between 6.0-7.5. Avoid waterlogged conditions.

{% elif result == 'chickpea' %}

Chickpea thrives in well-drained loamy soils with pH 6.0-8.0. Avoid heavy soils.

{% elif result == 'kidneybeans' %}

Kidney beans prefer well-drained sandy loam soils with pH 6.0-7.5.

{% elif result == 'pigeonpeas' %}

Pigeonpeas grow well in a wide range of soils but prefer well-drained loams with pH 5.0-7.0.

{% elif result == 'mothbeans' %}

Mothbeans are drought-tolerant and grow well in sandy loam soils with pH 7.0-8.5.

{% elif result == 'mungbean' %}

{% elif result == 'blackgram' %}

Blackgram grows well in loamy or sandy loam soils with pH 6.5-7.8.

Lentil prefers well-drained loamy soils with pH 6.0-8.0.

Cultivation Tips

{% if result == 'rice' %}
  • Maintain 2-3cm water depth during vegetative phase
  • Practice System of Rice Intensification (SRI) for higher yields
  • Apply FYM @ 5-10 tons/ha before planting
  • Use certified seeds for better germination
{% elif result == 'maize' %}
  • Plant spacing: 60cm between rows, 20cm between plants
  • Apply 120:60:40 kg NPK/ha for optimal yield
  • Practice crop rotation with legumes
  • Harvest when moisture content is 20-25%
{% elif result == 'chickpea' %}
  • Seed treatment with Rhizobium culture improves nitrogen fixation
  • Irrigate at flowering and pod formation stages
  • Harvest when 70-80% pods are mature
  • Thresh immediately after harvest to avoid shattering
{% elif result == 'kidneybeans' %}
  • Plant in rows 45cm apart with 10cm plant spacing
  • Apply phosphorus-rich fertilizer at sowing
  • Provide support for climbing varieties
  • Harvest when pods are fully developed but still green
{% elif result == 'pigeonpeas' %}
  • Intercrop with cereals for better land utilization
  • Apply 20:40:20 kg NPK/ha as basal dose
  • Practice ridge sowing in heavy rainfall areas
  • Harvest when 75% pods turn brown
{% elif result == 'mothbeans' %}
  • Sow in rows 30cm apart for better yield
  • Drought-tolerant but benefits from 1-2 irrigations
  • Harvest when 80% pods turn yellow
  • Thresh immediately after harvest
{% elif result == 'mungbean' %}
  • Treat seeds with Trichoderma viride @ 4g/kg seed
  • Apply 20:40:20 kg NPK/ha as basal dose
  • Harvest when 80% pods turn black
  • Dry to 10% moisture before storage
{% elif result == 'blackgram' %}
  • Treat seeds with Rhizobium culture before sowing
  • Apply 20:40:20 kg NPK/ha as basal dose
  • Harvest when 80% pods turn black
  • Thresh immediately after harvest
{% elif result == 'lentil' %}
  • Treat seeds with Rhizobium culture before sowing
  • Apply 20:40:20 kg NPK/ha as basal dose
  • Harvest when plants turn yellow and pods mature
  • Dry to 10% moisture before storage
{% elif result == 'pomegranate' %}
  • Plant in pits of 60cm³ filled with FYM and topsoil
  • Prune annually to maintain shape and productivity
  • Irrigate at 7-10 day intervals in summer
  • Harvest when fruits develop characteristic color
{% elif result == 'banana' %}
  • Plant suckers at 1.5m x 1.5m spacing
  • Remove excess suckers, maintain 3-4 per clump
  • Provide support to bearing plants
  • Harvest when fingers are plump but still green
{% elif result == 'mango' %}
  • Plant in pits of 1m³ filled with FYM and topsoil
  • Prune to maintain 3-4 main branches
  • Irrigate at 10-15 day intervals in non-rainy season
  • Harvest when shoulders develop and color changes
{% elif result == 'grapes' %}
  • Train vines on overhead trellis or bower system
  • Prune annually to maintain productivity
  • Irrigate at 7-10 day intervals
  • Harvest when berries are fully colored and sweet
{% elif result == 'watermelon' %}
  • Plant on raised beds for better drainage
  • Space plants 1.5m apart in rows 2m apart
  • Irrigate regularly, especially during fruit development
  • Harvest when tendril near fruit turns brown
{% elif result == 'muskmelon' %}
  • Plant on raised beds for better drainage
  • Space plants 1m apart in rows 1.5m apart
  • Irrigate regularly but avoid wetting foliage
  • Harvest when fruit slips easily from vine
{% elif result == 'apple' %}
  • Plant at 4m x 4m spacing in pits filled with FYM
  • Prune annually to maintain shape and productivity
  • Thin fruits to 1-2 per cluster for better size
  • Harvest when fruits develop characteristic color
{% elif result == 'orange' %}
  • Plant at 5m x 5m spacing in pits filled with FYM
  • Prune to remove dead and diseased branches
  • Irrigate at 10-15 day intervals in non-rainy season
  • Harvest when fruits develop orange color
{% elif result == 'papaya' %}
  • Plant at 1.8m x 1.8m spacing in pits filled with FYM
  • Maintain 1 male plant per 10 females for pollination
  • Irrigate at 5-7 day intervals in summer
  • Harvest when fruits develop yellow tinge
{% elif result == 'coconut' %}
  • Plant at 7.5m x 7.5m spacing in pits filled with FYM
  • Apply 1kg NPK mixture per palm annually
  • Irrigate young palms at 4-5 day intervals
  • Harvest when nuts are 11-12 months old
{% elif result == 'cotton' %}
  • Plant at 60cm row spacing with 30cm plant spacing
  • Apply 80:40:40 kg NPK/ha in split doses
  • Practice topping (removal of terminal bud) to control height
  • Harvest in 2-3 pickings when bolls open
{% elif result == 'jute' %}
  • Broadcast seeds at 5-6 kg/ha rate
  • Apply 40:20:20 kg NPK/ha as basal dose
  • Harvest at early pod stage for best fiber quality
  • Ret in clean water for 15-20 days for fiber extraction
{% elif result == 'coffee' %}
  • Plant at 2m x 2m spacing in pits filled with FYM
  • Prune annually to maintain productivity
  • Provide shade with silver oak or other trees
  • Harvest when berries turn red (cherry stage)
{% else %}
  • Test soil before planting for specific nutrient requirements
  • Monitor for pests regularly
  • Maintain proper irrigation schedule
  • Harvest at optimal maturity for best quality
{% endif %}
